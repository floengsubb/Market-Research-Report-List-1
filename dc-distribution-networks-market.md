<p><p>DC distribution networks can be categorized into three market types based on voltage levels: low voltage (up to 1 kV), medium voltage (1 kV to 35 kV), and high voltage (above 35 kV). Low voltage systems are commonly used in residential and small commercial applications, while medium voltage networks typically serve industrial and larger commercial sectors, supporting equipment like motors and transformers. High voltage networks are designed for long-distance transmission and distribution, efficiently delivering electricity across vast areas to minimize energy losses.</p></p>
