<p><p>The sucroglycerides market is segmented into two main types: monoglycerides and diglycerides. Monoglycerides, consisting of a single glycerol molecule attached to one fatty acid, are often used as emulsifiers in food and cosmetic products due to their excellent solubility and stability. Diglycerides, containing two fatty acids, serve similar functions but exhibit enhanced emulsifying properties. Both types are derived from fats and oils and play essential roles in enhancing texture, flavor, and shelf life across various industries.</p></p>
