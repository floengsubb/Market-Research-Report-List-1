<p><p>The veterinary thermometer market is primarily divided into two types: electronic and analog thermometers. Electronic veterinary thermometers utilize digital sensors to provide quick and accurate temperature readings, often featuring display screens and memory functions for easy tracking. In contrast, analog veterinary thermometers rely on traditional mercury or dial mechanisms, presenting readings through a calibrated scale. Both types serve essential roles in animal healthcare, with electronic models favored for their efficiency and ease of use, while analog models are valued for their simplicity and reliability.</p></p>
